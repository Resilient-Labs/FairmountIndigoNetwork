About the Fairmount Indigo Network

The Fairmount Line is the shortest commuter rail line in the MBTA system. It is the only line entirely within the City of Boston and has 8 stations with subway-like spacing. The Fairmount Line operates seven days a week. 45 minute frequency during weekday rush hours. 60 minute frequency during off-peak service and on weekends. Fares from 7 of 8 stations are the same as subway fares ($2.25 for single ride). Not integrated with the Charlie Card system. Fairmount Line riders do not receive a free transfer to/from a local bus or the subway, significantly reducing the range of destinations accessible with a single $2.25 fare. Fairmount Corridor residents have access to a range of high frequency local bus services. Local bus services run on-time less than 75% of the time. Fairmount Line has 94% on-time performance
